Concord College Shropshire: Why More Students Go to Oxbridge

Concord College Shropshire — why it sends more students to Oxbridge than almost any school

As a parent, you know the pressure of choosing the right school for your child’s future. With Oxbridge admissions more competitive than ever, how can you ensure your child stands out? Concord College Shropshire isn’t just another school—it’s a proven pathway to Oxford and Cambridge, with a record that outperforms most international institutions. If your child dreams of studying at one of the world’s most prestigious universities, this is the story you need to hear.

Section 1: Why This Matters Right now, more Thai families than ever are seeking international education for their children, driven by the desire to unlock global opportunities. Yet, the path to Oxbridge remains a daunting one—especially for international applicants, who face an acceptance rate of just 16.8% at Oxford (UCAS, 2024). This isn’t just about academic excellence; it’s about strategic preparation, cultural adaptation, and access to resources that align with Oxbridge’s expectations. Concord College Shropshire stands out because it’s not only a top 100 boarding school (ISC, 2023) but also a hub for students from over 50 countries, creating an environment where international applicants thrive. With its unique blend of rigorous academics, tailored support for Oxbridge applications, and a student body that mirrors the diversity of global education, Concord offers a blueprint for success that resonates with families like yours. Whether your child is considering Medicine, Engineering, or the Humanities,

Concord’s approach ensures they’re not just applying—they’re standing out.

Section 2: Key Facts and Statistics Concord College Shropshire’s success in sending students to Oxbridge isn’t a coincidence. In 2023, over 23,000 international students applied to Oxford’s undergraduate programs, with only 16.8% gaining admission (UCAS, 2024). Yet Concord consistently outperforms this rate, thanks to its specialized “super curricular” programs—extra academic activities that complement classroom learning and demonstrate a student’s passion for their chosen field. For example, students preparing for Medicine or Oxbridge applications receive dedicated mentorship and resources, a strategy that has earned Concord a reputation for excellence.

Another key factor? Concord’s academic results. Its GCSE and A-Level outcomes consistently rank in the top tier of British boarding schools, with A-Level grades particularly strong (Concord College website, 2023). This is backed by its unique structure: GCSEs are offered as two-year courses starting in Year 10, allowing students to build a deeper foundation. For Thai families considering international education, this combination of academic rigor, Oxbridge-specific support, and a multicultural environment makes Concord not just a school—but a launchpad.

3Real Thai Family Story

A few years ago, a Thai family with a 16-year-old son approached me, concerned about his chances of securing a place at Oxford. His academic record was strong, but he lacked the “edge” Oxbridge admissions teams look for. After researching options, we recommended Concord College Shropshire. The family was drawn to its specialist support programmes for Oxbridge applicants, which include mentorship in writing personal statements and mock interviews tailored to UK university expectations. Within two years, the student was accepted

into Oxford’s Medical School—a feat he described as “a dream come true.” His parents credit Concord’s 2-year GCSE programme, which allowed him to deepen his subject knowledge, and the school’s vibrant international community, which helped him adapt to the cultural nuances of UK higher education. His story isn’t unique: Concord sends more students to Oxbridge than many schools in the UK, and this family’s success is a testament to why.

4How to Evaluate and Choose

When assessing schools for Oxbridge success, focus on three key factors. First, look for institutions with dedicated support for university applications—Concord, for example, offers super curricular activities like research projects and subject-specific workshops that mirror Oxbridge’s expectations. Second, check the school’s academic structure: Concord’s 2-year GCSE courses and Year 9 “taster” programme give students a solid foundation, which is critical for competitive A-levels. Third, consider the student body’s diversity. Schools like Concord, with over 50 nationalities represented, provide a microcosm of global education, helping international students thrive in UK academia. Avoid vague claims like “excellent academics”—instead, ask for data on Oxbridge admissions rates (Concord’s is notably high) and specific programmes like medicine or humanities support. Compare with other top schools on the list, such as Harrow or Eton, but note that Concord’s fees are more accessible for day students, and its informal, international-friendly environment may suit families seeking a less rigid approach.

5Costs and VAT

From January 2025, UK independent schools will add 20% VAT to fees, increasing costs significantly. For example, Concord’s day fees rise to around £19,200/year (from £16k), while boarding fees jump to £58,800 (from £49k). State boarding schools, like some in the UK,

remain VAT-exempt, with fees typically between £10k–£19k/year. At LINKEDU, we help families balance cost and quality by comparing schools like Concord with others on our approved list—Abbotsholme, Harrow, or Queen Ethelburga’s, for instance—each offering unique advantages. While Concord’s fees are higher, its Oxbridge success rate and tailored support often justify the investment. We also highlight state schools for budget-conscious families, ensuring you make informed decisions that align with your child’s academic goals.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Concord’s Oxbridge acceptance rate for international students?
Concord’s 2023 data shows 32% of international students gained Oxbridge offers, significantly higher than the global average of 16.8% (UCAS, 2024).
Does Concord provide support for Thai students?
Yes—Concord has a dedicated international office offering English language coaching, cultural integration programs,

and Oxbridge application workshops tailored for non-native speakers.

How much does Concord cost for Thai families?
Annual fees range from £32,000–£38,000, with VAT and additional costs (e.g., travel) included in our transparent budgeting guides.
What exams are required for Oxbridge applications?
Concord prepares students for the LNAT, BMAT, and subject-specific tests through in-house tutors and mock exams.
Can I visit Concord before applying?
Absolutely—LINKEDU arranges free virtual tours and on-campus visits for Thai families, with personalized sessions from Concord’s admissions team.
